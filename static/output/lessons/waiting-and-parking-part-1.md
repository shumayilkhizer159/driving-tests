# Lesson 25: Waiting and parking - part 1

## Definitions

### To be stationary (or stanstill)

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_1.jpg) | Persons Being stationary or standstill means the time it takes for **someone to get in or out the car**.  This can happen quite quickly when it is only 1 person, but it can also take a few minutes, when, for example, many children have to get off the school bus. |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_2.jpg) | Goods To be stationary or standstill also means the **time it takes to load or unload goods**.  This can also take a while, for example when petrol is delivered or when moving house. |

### Parking

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_3.jpg) | If you stand longer than necessary to get someone in or out, or to load or unload a load, we are talking about: parking. |

### Stopped vehicle

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_4.jpg) | Sometimes a vehicle has to stop, for example because the traffic light is red or because there is a stationary traffic jam, or to give priority to another driver.  Stopping on public roads for these reasons is not considered waiting or parking. |

### Defective vehicle

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_5.jpg) | A vehicle that is broken down on the public roads and is no longer permanently driven is not a stationary vehicle or a parked vehicle. It's **a defective vehicle**.  A defective vehicle that cannot be parked in a safe place must be made recognizable to other road users by means of **the warning triangle**.  The traffic signs concerning waiting and parking do not apply to a defective vehicle. |

## General rules: where can you be stationary and park

### On the verge

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_6.jpg) | Is there place:   * **within a built-up area** on the **ground level verge**; * **outside the built-up area** on the **ground level or on the raised verge**;   then you have to park your car there. |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_7.jpg) | On some roads a thick white solid line has been painted to the right of the roadway. You are allowed to be stationary and park next to it. |

### Partly on the verge and partly on the carriageway

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_8.jpg) | If the verge is not wide enough, you partly park on the verge and partly on the carriageway.  But: you have to make sure that there is still **at least 1.5 meters of free passage for pedestrians** who use the verge, which is not the case here. |

### On the carriageway

![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_9.jpg) ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_10.jpg)

If you park on the carriageway, you do it as follows:

* as close as possible **to the edge of the carriageway**,
* right in the **direction of travel**.

So just standing still or parking somewhere on the roadway or along the left is forbidden.

* In a **single line**. Double parking or waiting is prohibited.
* Leave at least 1 meter of free space between two parked cars.

### One-way street

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_11.jpg) | On a one-way street, the car may be positioned both on the right or on the left. |

## Where is being stationary and parking prohibited - on the carriageway and on the verge

### On a crossing for pedestrians and cyclists

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_33.jpg) | You are not allowed **to be stationary** **or to park on the road and on the verge**:   * on a pedestrian crossing. * on a cyclists crossing. |

### On a junction (crossroads) without traffic lights

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_34.jpg) | You are not allowed **to be stationary** **or to park on the road and on the verge**:   * at intersections **less than 5 meters** from the extension of the adjacent edge of the cross carriageway. |

### On a junction (crossroads) with traffic lights

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_35.jpg) | You are not allowed **to be stationary** **or to park on the road and on the verge**:   * at a junction/crossroads with traffic lights **within 20 meters of the traffic lights**. |

### Traffic lights and traffic signs outside a crossroads/junction

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_36.jpg) | You are not allowed **to be stationary** **or to park on the road and on the verge**:   * **within 20 meters of the traffic lights** outside a crossroads/junction. * **within 20 meters of traffic signs**. |
| ![](images/waiting-and-parking-part-1_img_37.jpg) | **But**   * If the bottom of the road sign or light is **at least 2 meters high**, * and the top of the car with load does **not exceed 1 meter 65,**   then the 20 meters does not apply.  This car may therefore park or stop in that place because it does not interfere with the visibility of the sign. |
